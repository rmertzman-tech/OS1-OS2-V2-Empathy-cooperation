Welcome to OS2 Coordination Training

🎯 What You'll Learn

This simulator teaches you to recognize when moral disagreements stem from warrant incommensurability (OS1 conflicts) and how to shift to capability-based coordination (OS2) instead.

📚 Core Concepts

Operating System 1 (OS1): Coordination through Belief/Reason/Observation/Agreement. Requires people to share the same moral warrants (reasons why something matters). Works well within similar groups but fails across fundamental differences.

Warrant Incommensurability: Different PRFs generate different warrants for arguments, and these cannot be reconciled through logic alone. Example: "Suffering obligates response" (Care 4.5) vs. "Equal treatment is binding" (Fairness 4.8). Both are valid from their phenomenologies, but incompatible as universal principles.

Operating System 2 (OS2): Coordination through shared tasks + complementary capabilities. Doesn't require warrant agreement. Instead, identifies what each person's PRF enables them to do well and coordinates through capability complementarity.

The Goal: Expand your coordination capacity from ~10-15% of humanity (those with similar PRFs) to ~80-90% (anyone with relevant capabilities for shared tasks).

🔄 How This Works

Phase 1: You'll see 10 scenarios where two people with different PRFs are in conflict. You'll identify the warrant structures each person is using (their "OS1 arguments").

Phase 2: For each conflict, you'll practice OS2 reframing:

  • Identify shared constructor task (what do both want to accomplish?)
  • Map PRF-enabled capabilities (what can each person do well?)
  • Design coordination strategy (how to pool capabilities without agreement?)

Phase 3: Get detailed feedback on your analysis and see expert OS2 coordination strategies.

🔍 Your Task: Identify the warrant (moral reasoning) each person is using. Remember: warrants come from their PRF and may be incommensurable (incompatible but both valid).
